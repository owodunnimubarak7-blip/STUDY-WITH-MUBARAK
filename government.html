1. The party system that Nigeria operated in the aborted Third Republic is:

Correct: C - The Third Republic featured the SDP and NRC.

2. Delegated legislation can be regulated through:

Correct: B - Parliament oversees powers given to other bodies.

3. The main argument against the 1999 Constitution is that:

Correct: A - Critics argue it wasn't created by "the people."

4. Which of the following is a basic feature of the rule of law?

Correct: D - No one is above the law.

5. The collective views of the citizens on a specific national matter is:

Correct: C - It reflects the general stance of the populace.

6. The productivity of public corporations is believed to be enhanced when there is:

Correct: D - Autonomy leads to better efficiency.

7. NEPAD stands for:

Correct: D - Established in 2001 by the AU.

8. Under the Hausa pre-colonial administration, the office of the Prime Minister is usually occupied by:

Correct: B - The Waziri was the chief administrative officer.

9. Nigerian Ports Authority is an example of:

Correct: C - It is a government-owned commercial entity.

10. An agent in which the state undertakes direct political socialisation is:

Correct: B - Schools provide formal civic education.

11. The Nigerian Youth Movement pursued its objectives through the use of:

Correct: B - The 'Daily Service' was their main tool.

12. Formation of political parties in Nigeria is one of the features of:

Correct: D - The 1922 Clifford Constitution allowed elective principle.

13. The commander of the army under the Hausa/Fulani pre-colonial administration was:

Correct: B - The Madawaki headed the military.

14. The rationale behind the adoption of a federal system of government in Nigeria is to:

Correct: D - Federalism manages multi-ethnic differences.

15. Lawyers recruited into the civil service belong to the:

Correct: A - Specialized roles like doctors and lawyers are Professional.

16. A naturalised citizen can lose his citizenship if found guilty of:

Correct: A - Treason is a ground for deprivation of citizenship.

17. Shehu Shagari won the 1979 Presidential Election under:

Correct: B - National Party of Nigeria.

18. A form of government where the powers of the state is determined by the citizens is:

Correct: D - Government of the people, by the people.

19. As a non-aligned state, Nigeria attended the second meeting of the Non-Aligned Movement in:

Correct: C - Held in Cairo, Egypt.

20. The power of prerogative of mercy rests with the:

Correct: B - The President/Governor grants pardons.

21. The judgement that ceded Bakassi to Cameroon was delivered by:

Correct: D - The ICJ ruling in 2002.

22. ECOWAS was established in:

Correct: C - Signed on May 28, 1975 in Lagos.

23. The Nigerian Civil War was fought during the military regime of:

Correct: B - General Yakubu Gowon led from 1966-1975.

24. The fundamental objective guiding a nation's foreign policy is:

Correct: A - All foreign policy aims to benefit the nation.

25. The Aburi Accord was a popular phrase during the administration of:

Correct: C - Negotiated in Ghana before the civil war broke out.

112. The degree of centralization is high in a:

Correct: A - In a unitary system, power is concentrated in the central government.

113. A constitution that requires special procedures to amend is referred to as:

Correct: C - A rigid constitution cannot be changed by a simple majority vote.

114. A type of government which allows co-ordinate units to make laws is:

Correct: A - In a federation, power is shared between the center and states.

115. One feature of a Unitary State is:

Correct: C - Unitary states do not divide power between units via the constitution.

116. A characteristic of the Parliamentary System is that:

Correct: A - There is a "fusion" of executive and legislative powers in parliament.

117. In a confederation, the component units usually have the power to:

Correct: A - Units in a confederation often retain the legal right to withdraw.

118. A politically aware and active society has a:

Correct: B - Participatory culture involves active citizen involvement.

119. Fundamental Human Rights are best defined as:

Correct: A - These are basic rights guaranteed by the supreme law.

120. Which organ of government is responsible for interpreting laws?

Correct: C - The Judiciary settles disputes by explaining the law.
