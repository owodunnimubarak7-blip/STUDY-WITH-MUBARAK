STUDY-WITH-MUBARAK

Government Mastery: Questions 1 - 30

1. The degree of centralization is high in a:
Correct: A - In a unitary state, all power flows from the central government.
2. A constitution that requires special procedures to amend is:
Correct: C - A rigid constitution is difficult to change to prevent political instability.
3. A government that allows co-ordinate units to make laws is:
Correct: A - Federalism is based on power sharing between tiers of government.
4. One feature of a Unitary State is:
Correct: C - Sovereignty lies solely with the central authority.
5. Executive and legislative powers are fused in a:
Correct: A - In parliament, ministers are also members of the legislature.
6. A politically active society has a:
Correct: B - Citizens actively participate in elections and policy making.
7. Fundamental Human Rights are:
Correct: B - These rights are inherent and constitutionally protected.
8. A government of elected officials is:
Correct: A - Officials represent the will of the people who voted.
9. Devolution of power involves:
Correct: B - It is a form of decentralization.
10. Checks and Balances are meant to:
Correct: B - It keeps the Executive, Legislature, and Judiciary in balance.
11. In a confederation, units have the power to:
Correct: A - Units in a confederation are more powerful than the center.
12. The Head of State in Nigeria is also:
Correct: B - In the Presidential system, one person holds both roles.
13. Rule of Law implies:
Correct: B - No one is above the supreme law of the land.
14. The Judiciary's primary function is:
Correct: C - Courts interpret and apply the law to cases.
15. A Bicameral legislature has:
Correct: B - Example: Nigeria's Senate and House of Representatives.
16. Franchise means:
Correct: B - It is the political right to choose leaders.
17. Clifford Constitution (1922) introduced:
Correct: B - It allowed Nigerians to vote for the first time.
18. Richards Constitution (1946) created:
Correct: B - It divided the country into North, West, and East regions.
19. Macpherson Constitution (1951) was:
Correct: B - Nigerians were consulted at village and district levels.
20. Lyttleton Constitution (1954) established:
Correct: B - It marked the beginning of true federalism in Nigeria.
21. Nigeria became a Republic in:
Correct: B - Nigeria stopped having the Queen as Head of State.
22. The 1979 Constitution introduced:
Correct: B - Nigeria adopted the American style of government.
23. INEC stands for:
Correct: B - It is the body that organizes elections.
24. Gerrymandering is:
Correct: B - It is a political strategy to win unfairly.
25. The first Military Coup was in:
Correct: B - It led to the end of the First Republic.
26. Separation of Powers was by:
Correct: B - He popularized it in his book "The Spirit of Laws."
27. Public Opinion helps to:
Correct: B - Governments use it to know what citizens want.
28. Pressure groups want to:
Correct: B - They lobby those in power to change things.
29. Order of Precedence is:
Correct: B - It shows who is senior in government protocol.
30. Dictatorship is a government of:
Correct: B - Freedom is usually very limited in this system.
